EQUALIZER

an open data project focused on gender inequality in the workplace in Turkey

Both media and academic sources frequently highlight a significant disparity in wages and employment between men and women. With this research, we aimed to investigate whether this gap has widened over the past eight years.

We also know that in order to be employed, women need to prove themselves more than men with their education. Are women employed more compared to men when they have a higher education?

EQUALIZER is more than just a data project; it is a comprehensive investigation into the persistent and complex issue of gender inequality in Turkey's labor market. Through meticulous analysis and a focus on both employment and education, supported by robust data from TURKSTAT and EUROSTAT, we hope to contribute valuable insights that can inform future efforts to achieve true gender equality in the workplace.

Datasets

Original Datasets


To gather the necessary data for our research, we conducted an extensive search across various sources. We found particularly relevant data in the Turkish Statistical Institute (TÜİK-TURKSTAT) and EUROSTAT. TURKSTAT is the national statistical office of Turkey, responsible for collecting and analysing data on various aspects of Turkish society, including the economy, population, and employment. EUROSTAT is the statistical office of the European Union (EU). Its primary role is to provide statistical information to the institutions of the EU, enabling comparisons between countries and regions. But EUROSTAT does have data on Turkey as well. As Turkey is a candidate country for EU membership, EUROSTAT includes it in many of its statistical collections and publications.

    Analyses

    Quality Analysis


    EUROSTAT

    According to Assurance Framework of Eurostat (2019, Version 2), within 15 principles in total, they have five main principles for quality assurance:

    Quality Analysis
    Principle 11 Relevance European Statistics meet the needs of users.
    Principle 12 Accuracy and Reliability European Statistics accurately and reliably portray reality.
    Principle 13 Timeliness and Punctuality European Statistics are released in a timely and punctual manner.
    Principle 14 Coherence and Comparability European Statistics are consistent internally, over time and comparable between regions and countries; it is possible to combine and make joint use of related data from different data sources.
    Principle 15 Accessibility and Clarity European Statistics are presented in a clear and understandable form, released in a suitable and convenient manner, available and accessible on an impartial basis with supporting metadata and guidance.

    Indeed most of the principles were applied to the datasets, except the principle 15. We have spent a lot of time trying to find data on Turkey in Eurostat, not because it was absent, but it was not clearly underlined that it existed. Within the main user window, in some cases, Turkey was absent. But when we went to the list of parameters, we were able to see Turkey. From a researcher/user perspective, this was very tiring and deterring.

    TURKSTAT

    According to TURKSTAT’s declaration on their website, they are also committed to the same Quality Assurance Framework and the European Statistics Code of Practice by Eurostat:

    “TurkStat is committed to implementing a quality policy based on the European Statistics Code of Practice (ES-CoP), the European Statistical System Quality Assurance Framework (ESS-QAF), and other internationally recognized best practices determined by the European Statistical Office (Eurostat). In this context, TurkStat strictly adheres to and implements quality standards in statistical production processes by adopting the principle of continuous improvement and excellence in accordance with the ISO 9001:2015 Quality Management System Standard.”

    TURKSTAT too has mostly obeyed the quality standards they claim to commit. But it was also not the easiest job to find related data on their website. When we tried to search a keyword, even though it was in the name of the dataset, it was not showing any results. We had to specifically search the catalogues and titles within.

    Ethics analysis


    Both TURKSTAT and Eurostat adheres to the European Statistics Code of Practice, which sets the standards for producing high-quality, reliable, and impartial statistics. This Code ensures that institutions operates with professional independence, maintains rigorous methodological standards, and promotes transparency and accountability in all its statistical activities. By following these principles, both Eurostat and TURKSTAT guarantee the credibility and integrity of its data, supporting informed decision-making.

    Eurostat also states in Quality Declaration of the European Statistical System that “Our work is governed by professional independence, impartial treatment of all our users, objectivity, reliability, statistical confidentiality and cost-effectiveness. The development, production and dissemination of our statistics are based on sound methodologies, the best international standards and appropriate procedures that are well documented in a transparent manner. Our principles of quality are: relevance, accuracy, timeliness and punctuality, accessibility and clarity, as well as comparability and coherence.”

    TURKSTAT also states that “In Statistics Law of Turkey numbered 5429, the purpose of the foundation of our Institute is defined as to determine basic principles and standards concerning the production and organisation of official statistics, to compile and assess data and information, produce, publish and disseminate statistics on the areas that country needs, and to ensure coordination among institutions and organizations that are involved in the statistics process prescribed in the Official Statistics Programme.

    In the 4th article of the same Law, with the phrase ‘In order to improve the quality of official statistics, statistics produced within the scope of Official Statistics Programme shall be prepared and implemented in accordance with the principles of reliability, consistency, impartiality, statistical confidentiality, timeliness and transparency. The basic principles are as follows; ensuring the accuracy of official statistics, presenting the data to all users on the same time and on impartial base, respecting the principles of confidentiality and protecting the rights of the public to access the information.’ it is stated that basic principle is the reliability, confidentiality and accesability of data and statistical information. For this reason providing the security of information is extremely important for carrying out the activities of TURKSTAT’s reason for being, implementation of legal duty and corporate reputation.”


    Technical analysis


    Format:The data from Eurostat is available in various formats for download, such as TSV, Spreadsheet (.xlsx), and SDMX-CSV 1.0. The data format of TURKSTAT is spreadsheet (.xls).

    Metadata: Eurostat publishes a large amount of metadata. These provide the background information needed to fully understand and accurately interpret the European statistics released by Eurostat. This approach follows principle 15 of the European statistics code of practice, which states that data should always be made available with supporting metadata.

    Eurostat also has created and committed to “Reference metadata reporting standards”. According to their website: “Producers of official statistics must guarantee that European statistics are developed, produced, and disseminated on the basis of uniform standards and harmonised methods. Users of statistics must have access to relevant metadata, which describe the content and quality of statistical outputs. This helps them to interpret and use the statistics correctly. In order to respond to this need for harmonised reference metadata, Eurostat and the European Statistical System (ESS) developed the single integrated metadata structure (SIMS) to standardise their exchange and dissemination.”

    Since TURKSTAT also declares on their website that they are committed to the same Quality Assurance Framework and the European Statistics Code of Practice, they are committed to ensure the quality of the metadata by Principle 8 of the European Statistics Code of Practice.

    • Sustainability of the update the datasets over the time

    As of now, there are no plans to update the EQUALIZER Project further from our side. This decision stems from the project being a course requirement, with the current academic year being its designated timeline for completion. However, it is important to note the dynamic nature of the datasets we utilized.


    Continued Relevance of Original Datasets: The original datasets that served as the foundation for our analysis are part of an ongoing series maintained by their respective sources. These sources are committed to regularly updating the datasets, typically on an annual basis. This means that while our specific project may not receive direct updates, the underlying data will continue to evolve and reflect the latest information.

    Recommendations for Future Users: For users and researchers who wish to build upon our work or seek the most current data, we recommend the following:

    1. Refer to Original Sources: The datasets we analyzed are sourced from reliable repositories that ensure regular updates. By accessing these sources directly, users can obtain the latest versions of the datasets, which will include new data and potentially revised metadata.
    2. Check for Updates: Since the original sources update their datasets annually, it is advisable to check back periodically for the most recent data. This practice will help ensure that any ongoing or future research is based on the most up-to-date information available.
    3. Adapt and Extend: While we may not be updating the Open Access and Digital Ethics Project ourselves, we encourage other researchers to adapt and extend our work. By doing so, the research community can continue to benefit from the foundation we have laid, while also integrating new data as it becomes available.

    Commitment to Open Access and Digital Ethics: Throughout the project, we remained committed to the principles of open access and digital ethics. This commitment is reflected in our meticulous adherence to FAIR principles, ensuring that our data and findings are not only accessible but also reusable by others. By doing so, we aim to contribute to a culture of openness, collaboration, and ethical standards in the handling and dissemination of digital data.

    Findable
    The first step in (re)using data is to find them. Metadata and data should be easy to find for both humans and computers. Machine-readable metadata are essential for automatic discovery of datasets and services, so this is an essential component of the FAIRification process.

  • Our website is optimized for discoverability and is accurately indexed by search engines, enhancing its visibility.
  • Each dataset generated for our analysis is distinctly identified, ensuring that they can be easily traced and located.
  • We provide comprehensive metadata that includes essential details to facilitate the discovery of datasets and services automatically.

    • Accessible
    Once the user finds the required data, she/he/they need to know how they can be accessed, possibly including authentication and authorisation.
  • All data collected is available both on our platform and within the corresponding GitHub Repository.
  • We ensure that these datasets will remain available and downloadable, even if the original sources remove them in the future, guaranteeing sustained accessibility.
  • Our commitment to preserving and sharing valuable information supports transparency and enables continuous utilization for research and analysis.

    • Interoperable
    The data usually need to be integrated with other data. In addition, the data need to interoperate with applications or workflows for analysis, storage, and processing.
  • We meticulously characterize our combined datasets according to the DCAT-AP version 2.0.0 specifications.
  • We follow metadata guidelines that ensure interoperability with widely recognized formats such as SKOS, FOAF, PROV-O, and DCTerms.
  • This adherence to established standards enhances the coherence and structure of our metadata, facilitating seamless integration with different platforms and tools, and promoting broader accessibility and use of our datasets across diverse domains.

    • Reusable
    The ultimate goal of FAIR is to optimise the reuse of data. To achieve this, metadata and data should be well-described so that they can be replicated and/or combined in different settings.
  • The information presented on our website is available for unrestricted reuse, provided that users comply with the specified output license.
  • We encourage users to freely utilize and repurpose the data in accordance with the terms of the license agreement, fostering a collaborative and open approach to the dissemination of information.
  • By providing clear and detailed metadata, we support the replication and combination of our data in various contexts, maximizing its value for future research and applications.

    • All data and information on this website are published under the CC BY-SA 4.0 and CC BY-NC 4.0 licenses, adhering to the FAIR principles.

    Visualization

    To comprehensively understand the dynamics of gender inequality in Turkey's labor market, our visualizations delve into key aspects of employment and education. These figures are designed to illuminate the disparities and trends over the past eight years, providing a clear, data-driven perspective on the current state of gender equality in the workplace. They collectively offer a nuanced view of gender inequality in employment and highlight areas where efforts can be focused to bridge the gap.



    Employment by Sex - Unemployment by Sex (Turkey, 2014-2023)

    These figures illustrate the trends in employment and unemployment over the past decade, specifically focusing on gender disparities within the Turkish workforce. By delineating the employment rates of males and females from 2014 to 2023, it offers insights into the shifting dynamics of workforce participation, highlighting any disparities or convergences in male and female employment rates over the years.



    Employment Rate By Sex and Gender Employment Gap (Turkey, 2014-2023)

    This figure presents a nuanced analysis of the gender employment gap in Turkey, portraying the disparities in employment rates between males and females over the period of 2014 to 2023. By highlighting the magnitude and fluctuations of this gap over time, it offers valuable insights into the progress, stagnation, or regression in gender equality within the Turkish labor market.



    The Distribution of Employees by Education and Average Hourly Wage

    Offering a detailed breakdown of female and male employment across different educational attainment levels in Turkey, combined with the corresponding average hourly wages converted into Euros, these figures provide a granular understanding of the intersectionality between education, gender, and wages within the Turkish workforce. They illuminates any disparities or patterns in wage distribution among female workers based on their educational backgrounds.



    Hourly Wage by Educational Attainment Level and Sex

    This figure provides a comparative analysis of hourly wages among different educational attainment levels, disaggregated by gender, and converted into Euros for clarity. By mapping out the wage disparities between males and females across various educational backgrounds, it offers insights into the intersections of gender, education, and income within the Turkish labor market landscape.
